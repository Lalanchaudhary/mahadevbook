Venue Reports

Every ground tells a different cricket story.

From fresh surfaces to used strips, this page highlights the venue clues that most often change shot selection and bowling plans.

Fast outfields

Even well-timed pushes become boundaries, so captains often protect the square regions earlier than expected.

Dry surfaces

Grip and hold can reward cutters, finger spin, and patient batting that waits for length errors.

Short straight boundaries

Bowlers need clearer wide-line plans, while batters target clean extension down the ground.

Evening dew

The ball skids on more cleanly later, which can reshape defending plans and make yorker execution harder.

Surface Watch

Before first ball

Look at grass cover, visible cracks, and color variation. Those cues often hint at whether the pitch will stay true or slow down.

Boundary Map

Why dimensions matter

A ground with one short side can completely alter bowling angles and field settings, especially against batters who favor one arc.

Use venue context together with team style for the clearest match picture.
